You have found a book about cherries!

I am lying down on my belly, looking through the deep green
blades of grass. The weather is cooling down, but it has been
a hot day. I feel the first cold breeze blowing over my freshly
sunburned legs. It tickles; just like the ant walking over my
left forearm. My father enters the garden with a big
bowl of cherries. He shows me how you pull the stem off while
you keep the fruit in your mouth, and how you have to spit
out the pit. I try it and spit out the pit in my hand. There is still
a lot of pulp around the pit and it’s very slippery. It looks
beautiful; out of this world. A little planet where I would like to live.

Cherries used to be a delight I shared with my father. It is one
of my most vivid memories from when I was younger.
Maybe it’s because of the full tactile experience that cherries
evoke…

I haven’t been craving to eat cherries since I moved
out. The experience of eating a cherry has been replaced by
the hyperreal equivalent of the fruit: 🍒. A symbol that
represents both the male and female genitalia, sexuality and
the loss of innocence. The emoji keeps popping up. Some of
my favorite songs share a title with “cherry” in it. Is the tactile
experience I know reduced to a memory? Is my innocence
lost? How does one restore a relationship with a cherry?
